London, United Kingdom is exciting, busy, and layered with history, but it also feels livable because each neighborhood has its own rhythm, markets, parks, cafes, and local character. Visiting London means moving between grand landmarks, world-class museums, quiet side streets, and multicultural food spots, often all in the same day. The best season to visit is late spring or early autumn, especially May, June, or September, when the weather is milder and the city is comfortable for walking. Must-try foods include classic fish and chips, a Sunday roast, and a warm sticky toffee pudding or a good bakery pastry. Great activities for a student traveler include visiting the British Museum and other free museums, walking along the South Bank past major sights, and exploring neighborhoods like Camden, Notting Hill, or Shoreditch for markets and street life. Use an Oyster card or contactless payment for cheaper transport, and book major attractions in advance if you plan to visit paid sites. Bring a light waterproof jacket because rain can arrive suddenly, and choose accommodation near a Tube station so you can save time and money while getting around the city easily each day and avoid long, expensive cross-city commutes during busy hours.
